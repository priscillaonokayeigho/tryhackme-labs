## Phishing Analysis (Merry Clickmas)

### Lab Overview
This lab focused on identifying phishing attacks and understanding how attackers use social engineering techniques to trick users into clicking malicious links or revealing sensitive information.

### Tools / Environment
- Web browser
- TryHackMe AttackBox
- Simulated phishing emails

### Tasks Performed
- Reviewed phishing email samples
- Inspected sender addresses and email content
- Analyzed suspicious links without clicking them
- Identified common phishing indicators

### What I Learned
- How attackers use urgency, fear, and rewards to manipulate victims
- Common phishing red flags such as:
  - Spoofed sender addresses
  - Suspicious or shortened URLs
  - Poor grammar and unusual requests
- Why phishing is one of the most common attack vectors used today

### Security Relevance (SOC Perspective)
Phishing alerts make up a large percentage of SOC tickets. Understanding phishing techniques helps SOC analysts:
- Quickly triage and classify phishing emails
- Reduce false positives
- Escalate real threats before users are compromised
